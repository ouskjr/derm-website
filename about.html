From the founder

Slow skincare, by name.

I'm Magdana Machavariani — Lamazi is my one-person skincare practice, run out of homes along the Jersey Shore. I'm a licensed esthetician with the State of New Jersey, and I've never worked the spa-floor model. I drive to you. I work on your kitchen table or in your living room. I bring everything I'd use in a treatment room: the same lights, the same products, the same patience.

Lamazi means beautiful in Georgian, which is where my family is from. I named the practice after the kind of treatment my grandmother would have called real — slow, attentive, a small ritual rather than a service rendered.

Most weeks I see four or five clients. That's the whole list. I'd rather know your skin well than know a hundred skins lightly. If we work together, I'll write you a short routine to follow at home and check in between visits — that part doesn't cost extra.
